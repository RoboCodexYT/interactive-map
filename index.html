Climate in China

The northern regions of China, including Beijing and Inner Mongolia, experience cold winters with temperatures often dropping below freezing. Harsh winters impact transportation due to ice and snow. The southern regions, such as Guangzhou and Hong Kong, have hot, humid summers and mild winters. The rainy season can cause flooding, affecting transportation and agriculture. Western China, including Tibet and Xinjiang, faces extreme climates, rugged terrain, and travel difficulties.

Terrain in China

The Himalayas and Tibetan Plateau form natural barriers that limit overland travel. While modern high-speed railways improve accessibility, terrain challenges remain. The Yangtze and Yellow Rivers support agriculture and transportation, while the Gobi and Taklamakan Deserts present harsh conditions for travel and settlement.
