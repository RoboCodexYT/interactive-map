Travel Itinerary

Day 2: Beijing – The Great Wall & Summer Palace

  • Morning: Great Wall of China – Mutianyu (7:00 AM - 11:30 AM)
  • Lunch: Local restaurant (12:00 PM - 1:00 PM)
  • Afternoon: Summer Palace & Lama Temple (1:30 PM - 5:30 PM)

Day 3-5: Ulaanbaatar & Mongolian Exploration

  • Day 3: Arrival in Ulaanbaatar, visit Gandan Monastery, Sukhbaatar Square, and National Museum.
  • Day 4: Visit Terelj National Park, hike Turtle Rock, meet nomadic families.
  • Day 5: Explore Khustain Nuruu National Park, see wild Przewalski’s horses, return to Beijing.

China Climate

China's northern regions, including Beijing, experience freezing winters, requiring more heating and affecting transportation. Southern China, including Guangzhou and Hong Kong, has hot, humid summers and mild winters. Western China, such as Tibet and Xinjiang, has extreme climates with difficult travel conditions.

China Terrain

The Himalayas and Tibetan Plateau act as natural barriers, making travel difficult despite modern transportation. Major rivers like the Yangtze and Yellow Rivers support agriculture and travel, while deserts like the Gobi and Taklamakan create challenges for settlement.

Mongolia Climate

Mongolia has extreme temperatures, with harsh winters (-40°C) making travel difficult and hot, dry summers impacting agriculture. Short growing seasons lead to a reliance on livestock rather than crops.

Mongolia Terrain

The vast steppe supports pastoralism, with travel mostly done by horse, camel, or off-road vehicles. The Gobi Desert is arid and challenging for settlement. The Altai and Khangai mountains create natural boundaries, making some areas inaccessible in winter.
